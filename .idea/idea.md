The “Analyst Lens”

Geopolitical Intelligence Platform – Analyst Requirements Overview

Objective
Build an user based AI enabled geopolitical intelligence App/platform that collects, coordinates and synthesises global signals, produces timebound outlooks (24/48/72 hours), supports scenario development, and enables analysts to generate consistent, decisionrelevant outputs.

The platform should later connect these insights to internal risk and mitigation data to assess relevance and impact for ING.

What success looks like:


• Analysts spend less time collecting and more time assessing
  Analysts have thier own user login and working area seperate from everyone, where they can collect, organise and analyse information. They can also publish to a separate stakeholder-facing layer when ready.
• Trend outlooks are consistent, comparable and timeanchored
• Early signals are visible before they hit headlines
• Scenarios are faster to build, easier to reuse, and easier to explain
• Outputs are trusted, auditable and ready for senior consumption
Design principle
Start with a core Analyst Assistant to ensure quality, consistency and governance. Outputs generated by analysts can later be distributed through a separate stakeholder-facing layer.

Primary users

• Geopolitical analysts
• Risk analysts
• Security analysts (cyber and physical risk introduced later)
• Research teams
Core analyst needs

• Monitor predefined priority areas and detect emerging geopolitical trends (e.g. Middel East war, Gulf developments, China, Taiwan, US, South America, Europe, Hybrid warfare, tariffs, Elections, security threats, terrorism, Tech wars - developments and US/China strategic competition)
• Translate raw reporting into clear outlooks and trend signals
• Identify early warning signals and inflection points (including tile with 23/48/72 hour trends overview)
o Timeboxed geopolitical trend briefs
o Event and trend tracking with timeline views
• Produce structured, repeatable outputs (briefs, notes, bulletins) in standardised format
• Develop scenarios and understand impact pathways from data available in the solution
• Ability to interrogate existing data via naturallanguage questioning
• Visualise developments via conflict and impact heat maps
• Search content and generate tags (free or predefined)
• (Later) Link events and scenarios to internal risk, country and mitigation frameworks
• Links to broader economic and market indicators (e.g. oil prices)
• Ability to ingest market signals (e.g. Polymarket-style probability indicators) – and link directly to geopolitical trends/categories/output
• Preparation for later linkage to internal ING data
Core functions

Intelligence ingestion and organisation

• Ingest intelligence from approved external and internal sources
• Apply structured tagging (region, country, theme, sector, risk type, severity, confidence)
• Detect duplicate reporting and consolidate into single event cards
• Maintain event and theme timelines showing what changed, when and why
Trend and outlook engine (24/48/72 hours)

• Generate concise outlooks covering:
o Expected developments
o Key indicators/signposts
o Geopolitical and market implications
o Confidence level and rationale
• Enable analyst review, editing and approval before publication
Visual analytics

• Conflict heat map showing geographic intensity and event density
• Impact heat map translating events into secondorder effects (energy, shipping, sovereign risk, supply chains, sanctions, cyber spillovers)
• Drilldown from maps to events, sources and related analysis
Scenario generation

• Scenario builder producing:
o Baseline, upside and downside cases
o Triggers and warning indicators
o Time horizons and probability ranges (optional)
o Operational and market impacts
• Reusable scenario templates for consistency
AskAnything analytical interface

• Naturallanguage Q&A over:
o Published briefs
o Events and timelines
o Scenario library
• Answers must include:
o Clear summary response
o Sourcebased evidence and citations
o Recent changes where relevant
o Confidence/credibility assessment
o Basic sentiment or direction of risk
Publishing and analyst distribution

• Analystcontrolled publishing to internal feeds
• Searchable archive with filters (time, geography, theme, severity)
• Later extension to stakeholder distribution (Teams, digests)
External data

• Curated list of licensed intelligence sources
• Metadata capture (source, timing, geography, reliability)
Internal data (future integration)

• Country risk frameworks
• Risk registers (geopolitical, operational, thirdparty)
• Mitigation and contingency libraries
• Qualitative or proxy exposure indicators
